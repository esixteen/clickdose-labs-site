Questions

The things smart buyers ask first.

A designer sells you one guess, once. Their success metric is "the client approved it." Ours is your conversion rate.

The value is shots on goal: ten tested angles beat one polished guess, because nobody knows which message converts until real traffic votes.

The math: lifting a page from 1.5% to 2% conversion is a 33% reduction in CAC. Page Lab pays for itself on the first winning test. A static site never pays for itself at all.

  • Freelancer one-off: one page, one guess, no iteration.
  • Cheap page shop: volume without conversion strategy.
  • Traditional agency: strategy, at $5,000 a page and weeks of waiting per build.
  • Page Lab: copy, build, testing, and iteration in one monthly loop.

You can add as many requests to your queue as you want, whenever you want. We work them one at a time, in your priority order, so nothing sits half-finished. The moment one page ships, the next request starts. The queue never closes.

Most clients see 8 to 12 pages a month. The biggest driver is your feedback speed: the faster you review and respond, the faster the queue moves. Complex pages and extra revision rounds take longer, simple angle variants ship quickly.

A revision refines the direction set in the brief: copy tweaks, layout adjustments, swapping proof elements, tightening the offer. We run revision rounds until the page ships right. A new direction, like a different angle or a different audience, is a new request for the queue, and that is usually good news: it means you have a new hypothesis worth testing.

Your first page is live within about a week. After that, iterations move in days, not quarters. The exact pace depends on how quickly you turn around feedback: fast reviewers keep the loop tight.
