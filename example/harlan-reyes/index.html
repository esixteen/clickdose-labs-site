Why Timing Matters

Evidence disappears faster than you think.

Texas law generally gives you two years from the date of a crash to file an injury claim, and some deadlines are much shorter. But the practical deadline is sooner: camera footage gets deleted, skid marks fade, and witnesses' memories soften within weeks.

Talking to us today costs nothing and locks nothing in. It just protects your options while they still exist.

Common Questions

Answers before you even ask

How much does it cost to hire you?

Nothing up front, ever. We work on contingency: our fee is a percentage of what we recover for you, and if we recover nothing, you owe us nothing. The case review is free either way.

Do I really have a case?

That is exactly what the free review answers. As a rule of thumb: if you were hurt, the crash was not your fault, and it happened within the last two years, it is worth 15 minutes to find out. We tell you honestly, even when the answer is no.

The insurance company already made me an offer. Is it fair?

First offers are almost always low. It costs nothing to have us read it before you sign anything. Once you accept an offer and sign a release, your case is closed forever.

How long will my case take?

Many cases settle in a few months. Cases that go to trial take longer, often a year or more. We move as fast as the facts allow, and we keep you updated at every step, in plain English.
